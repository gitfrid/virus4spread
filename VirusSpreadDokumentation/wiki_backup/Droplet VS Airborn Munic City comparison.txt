## **Droplet VS Airborn comparison smaple**
<br>

The following example compares the droplet vs airborne theory.
**See also DR Clare Craig's -> Book Expired**

According to the droplet theory, viruses can only move locally at a small distance from an infected person. 
<br>In the airborne theory, virus particles are carried over greater distances with the air.

The Population density of the example corresponds to Munich City.

## **Settings**

<br>The parameter settings of the example are listed below.
<br>The different settings Droplet vs. Airborne are marked in yellow.
<br>The grid 2000x2000 points with 19200 persons corresponds to the density of the city of Munich.

**Important parameters Person:**
* Latency time 2 days
* Infection time 9 days
* Immunity period for re-infection 155 days
* Re-infection rate 11%.

**Virus parameters:**
* Duration of virus infection 
<br>Is the duration during which a virus can be infectious, i.e. does not decay.

* Number of viruses that multiply per iteration.
* Virus reproduction rate 100% 
<br>Means, during a person's infection phase, a new virus is reproduced at the person's
current coordinate, 
<br>approximately every other day/iteration.


The virus parameters are purely random assumptions as I have not found any data on them.
<br>The last two values simulate the assumption that viruses are reproduced at a certain number and rate over the infection period.
